"And in coding, L.L.M.s take away the drudgery and leave the human, soulful parts to you."

In this article, one of the interviewees (Anil Dash) says: "The reason that tech generally — and coders in particular — see L.L.M.s differently than everyone else is that in the creative disciplines, L.L.M.s take away the most soulful human parts of the work and leave the drudgery to you. And in coding, L.L.M.s take away the drudgery and leave the human, soulful parts to you."

I couldn't have articulated it better. The ideas are still mine. The motivation is there. Now it is just easier to go from idea to final product. I have also been able to speed up tasks in all my projects: developing, testing, maintenance.

True I wasn't very happy with ChatGPT specially at the beginning, but I feel very comfortable supporting my coding duties with Claude.

My golden rule still is:

Not a single line of code that I don't fully understand gets published.

As long as I can explain what's going on I am OK. This way I also make sure I keep learning.
