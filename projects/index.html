Drinking Water DBP Formation Model

A predictive model for disinfection byproduct (DBP) formation during drinking water chlorination. Given water quality parameters — dissolved organic carbon, bromide concentration, pH, temperature, chlorine dose, and contact time — the model predicts the expected yields of regulated trihalomethanes and haloacetic acids.

The model was trained on experimental and monitoring data and validated against independent datasets. The primary design goal was interpretability: the model's coefficients correspond to chemically meaningful relationships, making it useful for treatment engineers who need to understand not just what the prediction is but why it changes under different conditions.

Wastewater Organic Contaminant Removal Model

A QSAR (quantitative structure–activity relationship) model that predicts the removal efficiency of organic micropollutants in wastewater treatment based on molecular structure. Rather than requiring compound-specific experimental data, the model uses molecular descriptors computed from SMILES notation to estimate removal under conventional activated sludge treatment conditions.

This is particularly useful for evaluating compounds with limited or no empirical removal data — pharmaceuticals, industrial chemicals, and other emerging contaminants where the regulatory interest outpaces the experimental literature. The model follows OECD validation principles and includes applicability domain assessment.
